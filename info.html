In a Nutshell

Phishing is a scam in which potential victims are contacted in some way by someone planning to steal sensitive data such as personal information, credit card numbers, bank info, and other private information that is best kept to oneself.s

Types of Phishing

Email Phishing

Email phishing is any attempt to perform phishing attacks on non-specific individuals or parties, usually in massive quantities to increase chances that someone will fall for the scam. Email phishing scams are usually identifiable by unfamiliar contacts, suspicious attachments, and occasionally grammar mistakes that wouldn’t usually be made. Image credit: Maksim Goncharenok

SMS Phishing

SMS phishing is very similar to email phishing, with the notable difference that it is performed via text messages on cellular phones. Due to its similarity to email phishing, it contains similar warning signs, such as an unfamiliar contact, suspicious attachments or commands, or grammatical errors. Image credit: Adam AY

Voice Phishing

Voice phishing is any form of phishing that is conducted over phone calls. They usually use text-to-speech systems, most often fraudulently claiming that there has been an attack on the victim’s credit card or bank account. This is in an attempt to either get the victim to send information to the attacker or to connect the victim with a human caller who will attempt to use social engineering techniques to obtain data from the victim. Image credit: Alexander Andrews

Page Hijacking

Page hijacking is a phishing method where a hacker utilizes legitimate and trusted websites to gather data from users, like password information or more serious data, like banking details. Image credit: Mia Baumeister
